Stress Management

Deep Breathing Exercise

Sit comfortably and close your eyes. Slowly inhale through your nose for 4 seconds, hold for 4 seconds, then exhale through your mouth for 6 seconds. Repeat 4 to 6 times to lower your heart rate and ease stress.

Progressive Muscle Relaxation

Starting at your feet, tense the muscles for 5 seconds, then release for 10 seconds. Gradually move upward through your body, focusing on each muscle group to relieve physical tension.

Anxiety Relief

Mindfulness Meditation

Find a quiet space and sit comfortably. Focus on your breath and let thoughts pass without judgment. Stay with each breath for several minutes, gently returning your focus when distracted.

Grounding Technique

Reconnect with the present by identifying 5 things you can see, 4 things you can touch, 3 things you can hear, 2 things you can smell, and 1 thing you can taste. This helps divert focus from anxious thoughts.

Positive Affirmations

Choose positive affirmations like "I am calm" or "I deserve peace." Repeat them aloud or silently several times to help replace negative thought patterns with a positive mindset.

Depression

Journaling

Dedicate a few minutes each day to write down your thoughts and feelings. Reflect on experiences and moods to process emotions and identify patterns over time.

Physical Activity

Engage in moderate exercise like a brisk walk or yoga. Regular physical activity can help boost your mood, increase energy, and create a healthy routine.

Social Connection

Make time to connect with supportive friends or family. Even a short conversation or shared activity can uplift your mood and remind you that you're not alone.
